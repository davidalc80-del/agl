1. Listening

Listening Strategies – Before Watching the Video

How to Approach the Listening Task

In this session, you will watch the video “Mistakes at Work” from BBC Learning English. The video is realistic and includes natural expressions, spoken interaction, and useful phrases for the workplace. To help you understand it more easily, try using these strategies — based on the CEFR recommendations for B1+ learners:

  • Use your knowledge of the context. You know this is about making mistakes at work. Think of typical words you might hear: error, issue, apologise, fix.
  • Focus on the speaker’s intention, not every word. What is the speaker trying to do — admit a mistake, apologise, explain a solution?
  • Notice how people feel. Pay attention to tone, hesitation, or emotional words. This helps you understand attitudes and relationships.
  • Listen for keywords and repeated phrases. Common phrases like “I’m sorry that...” or “We’ve taken steps to...” will help you follow the ideas.
  • Use the activity structure to guide your listening. The video is divided into clear parts — focus only on one section at a time.
  • Listen more than once. First, get the general idea. Then, listen again to catch details and answer the questions.

Listening is not just about hearing — it’s about understanding the situation, the purpose, and the message. Use these strategies to get the most out of this session.

🎧 Listening Practice – Mistakes at Work

📺 Watch First

Before you start the activities, watch the video “Office English – Mistakes at Work” from BBC Learning English.



📝 The activities below are divided by sections of the video to help you focus on each part and understand the key expressions more easily.

✅ Activity 1 – Understanding the Problem (0:00–1:40)

Instructions: Listen to the first part of the video and choose the correct answer.

1. What feeling is mentioned at the beginning when receiving a message that says “Hi”?




2. According to the speaker, what is important when making mistakes?

Instructions: Listen and complete the phrases using the correct missing word.

✅ Activity 3 – Apologising and Responding (4:10–5:50)

Instructions: Match each phrase with what it does.

1. I’m really sorry about that.

2. Let me investigate the problem.

3. I’m sorry that you’re upset.

4. I’m sorry if you’re upset.
