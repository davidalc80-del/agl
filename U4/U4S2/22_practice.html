2.2. Practice

Exercise 1 – Multiple Choice: Phrasal Verbs

Instructions: Select the correct phrasal verb to complete each sentence using the dropdown menu, then click "Check Answers" to see feedback.

  • 1. We need to _____ the complaint before it escalates further.
  • 2. Can you _____ me to the technical support team, please?
  • 3. Let’s _____ the details of the project timeline tomorrow.
  • 4. It’s challenging to _____ why the payment didn’t go through.

💡 Tip: Use “deal with” for handling issues and “figure out” for solving or understanding problems.

Exercise 2 – Match the Expression to the Context

Instructions: Select the correct functional expression for each workplace situation, then click "Check Answers" to see feedback.

Situation Expression Feedback
You’re addressing a client who has been waiting for an update.
You need to involve a supervisor to resolve an issue.
You’re actively addressing a technical glitch reported by a user.
You need more time to provide a detailed response.

💡 Tip: These expressions are great for showing empathy and professionalism in customer-facing roles.

Exercise 3 – Fill in the Blank

Instructions: Select the correct phrasal verb from the dropdown to complete each sentence. One option is extra. Click "Check Answers" to see feedback.
Options: deal with – follow up – figure out – sort out – put through

  • 1. I’ll _____ you to the department handling returns.
  • 2. We need to _____ the budget issues before the next meeting.
  • 3. Can you _____ with the supplier to confirm the order details?
  • 4. It’s important to _____ the root cause of the delay.

💡 Tip: “Sort out” is great for resolving logistical or operational issues, while “put through” is specific to phone connections.
