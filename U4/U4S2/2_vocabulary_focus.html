2. Vocabulary Focus

Vocabulary and Practice – Responding to Problems at Work

Why This Vocabulary Matters

When a problem arises at work — a missed deadline, a technical glitch, or an upset client — your ability to respond clearly and professionally makes all the difference. This section equips you with practical vocabulary to describe issues, propose solutions, and reassure colleagues or clients, ensuring you sound confident and competent.

You’ll focus on two key areas: phrasal verbs for taking action and functional expressions for empathetic and professional communication. These tools are designed for real-world workplace scenarios, such as emails, phone calls, or team meetings.

  • Explain problems clearly and concisely
  • Collaborate with colleagues to resolve issues
  • Communicate politely with clients or stakeholders
  • Build confidence in handling workplace challenges
