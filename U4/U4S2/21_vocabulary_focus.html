2.1. Vocabulary Focus

Vocabulary Focus – Dealing with Problems at Work

Why This Vocabulary Matters

Navigating workplace challenges — like delays, complaints, or technical issues — requires clear and professional communication. This vocabulary equips you with practical expressions and phrasal verbs to address problems confidently, whether in meetings, emails, or customer interactions.

In this session, you’ll learn two key sets of language tools: phrasal verbs for problem-solving and functional expressions for supportive communication.

🎧 Complementary Audio – Guided Explanation
This audio supports you as you go through the lesson. Listen to it for a step-by-step explanation with spoken examples. It’s especially helpful if you prefer learning by listening or want to review while doing other tasks.

🔧 Phrasal Verbs for Solving Problems

These phrasal verbs are commonly used when addressing workplace issues. They’re professional yet conversational, perfect for emails, calls, or team discussions.

Phrasal Verb Meaning Example
deal with To handle or manage a problem We need to deal with the client’s concerns promptly.
sort out To resolve or fix an issue Let’s sort out the scheduling conflict today.
follow up To check or continue communication I’ll follow up with the team tomorrow morning.
figure out To understand or solve something We’re trying to figure out why the system crashed.
put through To connect someone via phone I’ll put you through to the support team.

💡 Tip: Use “sort out” and “figure out” in casual discussions, but avoid overuse in formal emails.

💬 Functional Expressions for Supportive Communication

These expressions help you respond professionally and empathetically when addressing complaints or issues, showing you’re engaged and committed to finding solutions.

Expression When to Use Example
We’re working on it. To show a solution is in progress We’re working on it and will update you soon.
I’ll escalate this. To indicate you’ll pass the issue to higher authority I’ll escalate this to the manager for review.
Let me get back to you. To request time before responding Let me get back to you after checking the details.
Thanks for your patience. To acknowledge someone’s wait Thanks for your patience while we resolve this.

💡 Tip: These phrases work well in customer service or team communication to build trust and clarity.

🧠 How to Use This Vocabulary

Incorporate these expressions into your daily work communication:

  • Emails or phone calls with clients or colleagues
  • Team meetings to discuss and resolve issues
  • Customer service scenarios to address complaints

Practice these phrases in the activities and writing tasks of this session. Pay attention to how they’re used in the “Mistakes at Work” video and try them in your own conversations.
