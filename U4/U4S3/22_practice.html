2.2. Practice

Writing Practice – Request for Support (B2B)

🛠 Task Overview

Context: You work in the purchasing department. You’ve received defective goods or experienced a delay. Write an email to the supplier explaining the problem and requesting a solution.

Focus:

  • Grammar: Use second and third conditionals to describe hypothetical situations or past mistakes.
  • Function: Describe the problem clearly and ask for support or compensation.
  • Tone: Keep the email formal and professional, as this is a business-to-business (B2B) communication.

Example:
If the order had been double-checked, the mistake would have been avoided. Please let us know how you plan to fix the issue.

📝 How to Complete the Task

Write a short email (100–150 words) to the supplier. Post the your email on the corresponding forum. Follow these steps:

  • Greeting: Use a formal opening, e.g., “Dear [Supplier Name/Team].”
  • State the Problem: Explain the issue (e.g., defective goods or delay), using a second or third conditional to reflect on what could have been different, e.g., “If the items had been inspected, we would have avoided this issue.”
  • Request a Solution: Politely ask for action, using phrases like “Please let us know” or “We would appreciate your prompt response.”
  • Closing: End formally, e.g., “Thank you for your attention. Best regards, [Your Name].”

💡 Tip: Use Unit 4 vocabulary like “deal with” or “sort out” to sound professional, and keep sentences clear and concise.
