1. Use of English

Use of English – Third and Mixed Conditionals

Reflecting on Past Situations and Their Consequences

At work, things don’t always go as planned. A missed deadline, a technical error, a forgotten task… In these moments, we often think about what could have happened if the situation had been different. That’s when third and mixed conditionals are useful.

In this section, you’ll explore how to talk about:

  • Imagined alternatives to past actions (third conditional)
  • How a past action affects the present (mixed conditional)
  • Real workplace situations where this grammar is essential

📚 What You’ll Do

You’ll work through three parts:

  • 1️⃣ Diagnostic Test
    Find out what you already know — and where you need more practice — by identifying and correcting typical conditional structures.
  • 2️⃣ Grammar Focus
    Explore third and mixed conditionals from a cognitive perspective: how we mentally connect events across time, and how language reflects imagined consequences and responsibility.
  • 3️⃣ Guided Practice
    Apply your knowledge through real work-based examples. You’ll notice how the grammar is used in professional communication and learn to use it yourself with more confidence.

🧠 This section will help you develop accuracy, clarity, and flexibility when describing problems, making polite suggestions, or reflecting on decisions — key skills in any workplace.
