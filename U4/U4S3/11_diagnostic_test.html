1.1. Diagnostic test

Diagnostic Test – Third and Mixed Conditionals

🧠 Get Ready to Check Your Grammar!

Before you move on to real workplace tasks, take a moment to check how confident you are using conditional sentences that describe past situations and their consequences.

This diagnostic test focuses on:

  • ✅ Third conditional – to imagine how things could have been different in the past
  • ✅ Mixed conditionals – to connect a past situation to a present result

This isn’t a test for marks — it’s a tool to help you understand your progress and learning needs.

🎯 What’s the Goal?

To help you decide if you can use third and mixed conditionals confidently — or if you need a quick review before using them in writing, emails or spoken reflections at work.

📘 How Does It Work?

  • ✍️ Complete 20 sentences by choosing or writing the correct form.
  • 🔎 Check your answers.
  • 🧠 Reflect on how meaning changes with each structure.

🧮 Scoring

  • 80% or more → Great! You’re ready for real tasks and confident language use.
  • Less than 80% → No worries! Review the grammar section and practise with the activities — you’ll feel more secure in no time.

🌟 What You’ll Gain

This activity helps you develop:

  • 🎓 Competence to Learn to Learn – Identify what you’ve mastered and what needs review
  • 🌱 Lifelong Learning Competence – Gain awareness of how to keep growing as a user of English

🚀 Let’s begin and see how much you already know!

Diagnostic Test – Third and Mixed Conditionals

Instructions: Complete the test by filling in the blanks, choosing the correct option, or correcting errors. There are 20 items. Click "Check Answers" for each part to see feedback. At the end, check your score. If you score 80% or higher (16/20), you can skip this grammar point.

Part A – Complete the Sentence

Instructions: Type the correct verb forms to complete each third or mixed conditional sentence. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 1. If we ______ (schedule) the delivery, the client ______ (not/wait) now.
  • 2. If I ______ (attend) the meeting, I ______ (understand) the plan yesterday.
  • 3. If they ______ (test) the product, it ______ (not/fail) last week.
  • 4. If she ______ (review) the data, we ______ (not/be) confused now.
  • 5. If we ______ (hire) more staff, we ______ (complete) the project on time.
  • 6. If I ______ (back up) the files, we ______ (not/lose) the data now.

💡 Tip: Use “had + past participle” for the ‘if’ clause; use “would have + past participle” for past results or “would + base verb” for present results.

Part B – Choose the Correct Option

Instructions: Select the correct option to complete each sentence. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 7. If we had followed the plan, we _____ the deadline.
  • 8. If I had studied the manual, I _____ how to fix this now.
  • 9. If they had shipped the order, the client _____ now.
  • 10. If we had prepared the report, we _____ it yesterday.
  • 11. If I had confirmed the booking, we _____ a room now.
  • 12. If he had listened to advice, he _____ the mistake last week.
  • 13. If she had contacted the supplier, we _____ short of materials now.

💡 Tip: Third conditionals use “would have + past participle” for past results; mixed conditionals use “would + base verb” for present results.

Part C – Find and Correct the Mistake

Instructions: Each sentence has one grammar error. Type the corrected sentence in the text box. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 14. ❌ If I would have prepared better, I wouldn’t be nervous now.
  • 15. ❌ If we had informed the team, we won’t be behind schedule.
  • 16. ❌ If she checked the document, it would have been perfect yesterday.
  • 17. ❌ If you had been more careful, you wouldn’t made the error.
  • 18. ❌ If they would have tested the system, it wouldn’t crash now.
  • 19. ❌ If I had saved the file, I won’t be redoing it now.
  • 20. ❌ If he had arrived on time, we would finished the meeting early.

💡 Tip: Look for errors in verb forms or tense consistency in the ‘if’ or result clauses.
