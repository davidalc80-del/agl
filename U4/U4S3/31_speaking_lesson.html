3.1. Speaking Lesson

Speaking Lesson – Talking About Problems and Solutions at Work

Why This Matters

In professional settings, discussing workplace issues—whether with colleagues, supervisors, or clients—is a critical skill. Being able to clearly describe what went wrong, reflect on alternative actions, and propose actionable solutions demonstrates competence and professionalism. This lesson equips you with the tools to speak confidently about workplace challenges, using the grammar and vocabulary from Unit 4 to structure your ideas effectively.

By practicing these skills, you’ll be better prepared for real-world scenarios, such as explaining a project delay in a team meeting or offering advice to a coworker, enhancing your ability to communicate with clarity and poise.

Tip: Focus on being clear and professional, even in high-pressure situations, to build trust and credibility.

What You’ll Learn to Do

This lesson will help you develop the following speaking skills:

  • Describe a real or imagined workplace problem with precision and clarity.
  • Use conditional sentences (e.g., second and third conditionals) to reflect on past mistakes or suggest improvements.
  • Offer polite and constructive advice to coworkers to foster collaboration.
  • Speak with confidence and a clear structure, even when discussing challenging or stressful topics.

These skills are essential for professional communication, enabling you to contribute effectively in meetings, discussions, or client interactions.

Strategies for Effective Speaking

To help you speak clearly and professionally, this lesson incorporates CEFR-informed strategies for spoken production, such as planning, organizing ideas, and using connectors for coherence. Below are practical strategies to guide your preparation and performance:

Plan Before You Speak

Organize your thoughts using a simple outline to ensure your message is clear:

  • What happened? (Describe the problem, e.g., a delayed shipment.)
  • Why did it happen? (Explain the cause, e.g., a miscommunication.)
  • What was the result? (State the impact, e.g., a missed deadline.)
  • What could have been done differently? (Reflect using conditionals.)
  • What can we do next time? (Propose solutions or advice.)

Planning helps you stay focused and communicate your ideas logically.

Use Helpful Connectors

Connectors make your speech flow naturally and help your listener follow your ideas. Use these expressions:

Purpose Expressions
Describe the problem One issue we had was… / The problem started when…
Explain the cause It happened because… / The main cause was…
Describe the result As a result… / This affected… / It delayed…
Reflect on the past If we had… we would have… / It would have helped if…
Give advice Next time, you could… / If something similar happens, I suggest… / You should have…

Speak Clearly and Steadily

To communicate effectively:

  • Prioritize clarity over perfection—focus on getting your message across.
  • Pause briefly between ideas to give your listener time to process.
  • Emphasize key points by stressing important words (e.g., “The delivery was late, so we missed the deadline.”).

Practicing these techniques will help you sound confident and professional.

Tip: Practice these strategies with a partner or by recording yourself to refine your delivery.

Useful Phrases for Workplace Discussions

These phrases, drawn from Unit 4, will help you describe problems, reflect on actions, and propose solutions in a professional manner:

Category Phrases
Describing what went wrong There was a problem with the delivery / system / communication.
The incident occurred during the morning shift.
It wasn’t reported in time / It was overlooked.
Expressing regret or reflection If we had followed the procedure, this wouldn’t have happened.
It would have helped if someone had checked the labels.
We should have confirmed the details beforehand.
Giving advice to a coworker If I were you, I would…
You could try to… next time.
Next time, don’t hesitate to ask for help.
It’s a good idea to double-check before confirming anything.
Proposing solutions Next time, we can… / We should… / I recommend…
To avoid this in the future, we need to…
Let’s make sure we… before we…

Tip: Memorize a few phrases from each category to use them naturally during discussions.

Practice Tips for Success

To prepare for the speaking tasks, follow these steps to build confidence and fluency:

  • Practice out loud with a partner or alone to get comfortable with the phrases and structure.
  • Use scenario cards or real-life examples (e.g., a missed deadline, a technical error) to make practice relevant.
  • Record yourself speaking and listen back to evaluate clarity, structure, and tone. Ask: Is my message clear? Do I sound professional?
  • Prepare brief notes to guide your speaking, but avoid reading directly to maintain a natural delivery.
  • Incorporate Unit 4 vocabulary (e.g., “deal with,” “sort out,” “follow up”) to enhance fluency.

These techniques will help you refine your speaking skills and prepare for both practice and evaluated tasks.

Tip: Time yourself to ensure you can explain a problem and solution in 1–2 minutes, a common expectation in workplace discussions.

Preparing for the Practice Task

The speaking practice task will involve discussing a workplace problem, reflecting on what went wrong, and proposing solutions. To prepare:

  • Choose a scenario (e.g., a delayed delivery, a miscommunication with a client).
  • Follow the planning outline: describe the problem, cause, result, reflection, and solution.
  • Use conditionals to reflect (e.g., “If we had checked the schedule, we would have avoided the delay.”) and advice phrases (e.g., “Next time, I suggest confirming the details.”).
  • Practice delivering your response in a clear, professional tone, using connectors to organize your ideas.

This practice will build your confidence for real-world workplace conversations and prepare you for any evaluated speaking tasks.

Tip: Practice with a peer on the platform’s discussion forum to get feedback and improve.

Key Takeaways

This lesson is about more than grammar—it’s about mastering a critical communication skill. By learning to describe problems, reflect on past actions, and propose solutions clearly and professionally, you’ll be better equipped to handle workplace challenges and contribute to your team’s success.

Proceed to the practice section, select a scenario, and start speaking. Your ability to communicate effectively will set you apart in any professional environment.
