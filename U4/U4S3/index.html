Unit 4: Problem solving at work - Session 3

🧭 Session 3 – Reflecting, Explaining, and Responding

Why This Matters

💼 When workplace problems arise, solving them is only part of the job—reflecting on what happened and communicating clearly with others is just as important. This session equips you with the skills to describe issues, analyze causes, and propose solutions professionally, whether in writing or speaking.

💡 Tip: Clear communication builds trust and shows professionalism.

✅ What You’ll Do

You’ll develop grammar, writing, and speaking skills to handle workplace challenges effectively. In this session, you will:

  • Review third and mixed conditionals to discuss past events and their present impacts.
  • Practice written communication to request support from a supplier or respond to a client.
  • Build oral communication skills to reflect on incidents and give advice with clear structure and tone.

These activities, aligned with CEFR communication strategies, prepare you for professional interactions like emails or team discussions.

💡 Tip: Focus on clarity and structure to communicate confidently.

🚀 Getting Started

This session includes lessons on grammar, writing, and speaking, plus practice tasks to apply your skills, such as writing to a supplier and advising a coworker. Start with the grammar lesson to review conditionals, then explore the writing and speaking sections to build your communication skills.

💡 Tip: Use Unit 4 vocabulary like “sort out” or “escalate” to sound professional.
