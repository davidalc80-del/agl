3.2. Practice

Speaking Practice – Giving Advice to a Coworker

Task Overview

Context: A coworker has described a problem they faced during their shift and is asking for your advice on what they could have done differently and how to handle similar situations in the future.

Task: Individually, select a workplace scenario provided on the platform (e.g., via a scenario card or text description). Record a spoken response as if giving advice directly to your coworker. In your response:

  • Acknowledge the problem by summarizing it briefly to show understanding.
  • Explain how the situation could have been handled differently, using past-focused structures like third conditionals or “should have.”
  • Offer advice or solutions for the future, using first or second conditionals or phrases like “you could.”
  • Use a polite, supportive, and professional tone to maintain a collaborative approach.

This task develops CEFR-informed spoken production skills, such as organizing ideas clearly and delivering advice constructively, preparing you for professional workplace communication.

Tip: Imagine you’re speaking to a colleague in a supportive team discussion to keep your tone professional and encouraging.

Language Focus

Your response should incorporate these language elements from Unit 4:

  • Third Conditional: Reflect on alternative past actions (e.g., “If you had checked the inventory, the error might have been avoided.”).
  • First and Second Conditionals: Provide advice for future situations (e.g., “If this happens again, you could…” or “If I were you, I would…”).
  • Workplace Vocabulary: Use problem-solving terms like “deal with,” “sort out,” “escalate,” or “follow up” to sound professional and natural.

These language tools help you analyze past issues, propose actionable advice, and demonstrate empathy in a workplace context.

Tip: Use conditionals to add depth, but prioritize clear, simple advice to ensure your message is easy to follow.

How to Complete the Task

Prepare and record a short spoken response (1–2 minutes) for submission on the platform. Follow these steps:

  • Step 1 – Select a Scenario: Choose a workplace scenario from the platform’s resources (e.g., a delayed shipment, a client complaint, a technical issue). Read the description carefully, noting the problem, cause, and impact.
  • Step 2 – Plan Your Response: Organize your thoughts using this structure:
    • Acknowledge the problem (e.g., “I understand the client was frustrated due to the wrong order.”).
    • Reflect on the past (e.g., “If you had confirmed the details, the mistake could have been prevented.”).
    • Offer future advice (e.g., “Next time, you could double-check with the team to avoid errors.”).
  • Step 3 – Practice: Rehearse your response out loud to ensure clarity and a steady pace. Focus on a supportive tone and natural delivery.
  • Step 4 – Record and Submit: Record your response using the platform’s audio tool. Use brief notes to guide you, but speak naturally rather than reading. Submit the recording for feedback or self-review.

Example Response

I understand the issue with the delayed delivery caused a lot of stress during your shift. If you had contacted the logistics team earlier, the problem might have been resolved faster. In the future, I suggest escalating delays immediately to avoid client complaints. If something like this happens again, you could also keep the client updated to manage their expectations.

Tip: Listen to your recording to check for clarity, politeness, and structure before submitting.

Tips for Success

To deliver an effective response:

  • Begin with a supportive statement to show empathy (e.g., “That sounds like a challenging situation.”).
  • Use conditionals sparingly to avoid sounding critical; focus on constructive, forward-looking advice.
  • Incorporate Unit 4 vocabulary (e.g., “sort out,” “we’re working on it”) to enhance fluency and relevance.
  • Practice with multiple scenarios to build confidence in handling different workplace issues.
  • Ensure your recording is clear by speaking at a steady pace and pausing briefly between ideas.

These strategies will help you produce a professional, supportive response that mirrors real workplace communication.

Tip: Aim for a 1–2 minute response to keep it concise, as expected in professional discussions.
