3. Speaking

Speaking – Reflecting and Responding to Problems at Work

Why This Matters

Workplace issues like delivery delays or client complaints are common. How you discuss them—with clarity and professionalism—sets you apart. This section helps you describe problems, reflect on what went wrong, and suggest solutions using Unit 4 grammar and vocabulary.

💡 Tip: Speak clearly to build trust in workplace conversations.

🎯 What You’ll Practice

You’ll hone your speaking skills through a practice task where you give advice to a colleague about a workplace issue, preparing you for situations like supporting a coworker or discussing incidents with a manager. You’ll focus on:

  • Structuring ideas clearly.
  • Using conditionals to reflect on causes and consequences.
  • Expressing feedback or regret constructively.
  • Maintaining a calm, professional tone.

These skills, aligned with CEFR spoken production strategies, will boost your confidence in professional settings.

💡 Tip: Practice to sound confident and supportive.

🚀 Getting Started

The speaking section includes a lesson to learn key strategies and phrases, followed by a practice task where you’ll record advice for a coworker. Start with the speaking lesson to prepare, then tackle the practice task to apply your skills.

💡 Tip: Outline your response (problem, reflection, advice) for clarity.
