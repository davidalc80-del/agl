2. Use of English

Use of English: Conditional Sentences 🧠

How to Talk About Consequences and Possibilities

In the workplace, actions often have consequences. If someone misses a deadline, the team feels the pressure. If colleagues communicate well, everything works more smoothly. These are examples of conditional sentences.

We use conditionals to:

  • State general rules or routines (Zero Conditional)
  • Talk about possible future situations (First Conditional)
  • Imagine alternative scenarios or give advice (Second Conditional)

This section will help you learn how to use all three forms to describe real workplace problems and propose effective solutions — just like in the reading activity.

Let’s discover how conditionals can make your English clearer, more professional, and more useful at work.
