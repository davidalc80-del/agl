2.3. Practice

Grammar Practice – Conditionals 🧠

Activity 1: Understanding the Speaker’s Perspective 🌟

Read each sentence and choose how the speaker sees the situation.

💡 Use Zero Conditional for general facts, First Conditional for real future possibilities, and Second Conditional for hypothetical situations.

  • 1. If employees feel supported, they do better work.

  • 2. If we finish early, we’ll help the marketing team.

  • 3. If I had more free time, I would do a professional course.

Activity 2: Same Situation, Different View 🌟

Match each version of the situation with the mental frame it expresses.

💡 Use Zero Conditional for general facts, First Conditional for real future possibilities, and Second Conditional for hypothetical situations.

  • 4. A. If you arrive late, the team starts without you.

  • 5. B. If you arrive late, the manager will talk to you.

  • 6. C. If you arrived late every day, people would stop trusting you.

Activity 3: Fill the Gap: Understanding Use 🌟

Complete each sentence with the correct form of the verb. Think: is it a rule, a plan, or an imaginary scenario?

💡 Use Zero Conditional for general facts, First Conditional for real future possibilities, and Second Conditional for hypothetical situations.

  • 7. If people ___ clearly, mistakes happen.

  • 8. If the company shares more information, people ___ safer.

  • 9. If employees had more control, they ___ more motivated.

Activity 4: What Kind of “if” is it? 🌟

Read each sentence. What does “if” express? Choose all that apply.

💡 Use Zero Conditional for general facts, First Conditional for real future possibilities, and Second Conditional for hypothetical situations.

  • 10. If teams cooperate, stress goes down.

  • 11. If I worked in that department, I’d try a different method.

  • 12. If we reduce overtime, workers will be more focused.

Complete all activities to see your total score.
