3.1. Vocabulary Focus

Vocabulary Focus: Workplace Problems and Solutions 🛠️

Why This Vocabulary Matters

No job is perfect — and from time to time, things go wrong. A delay, a mistake, a lack of support or miscommunication can create tension, reduce efficiency, or even affect people’s well-being. But knowing how to describe these problems — and how to talk about possible solutions — helps you become a more effective and professional communicator.

In this session, you’ll learn vocabulary to:

  • Identify common workplace problems and their causes
  • Talk about the effects of problems on teams, clients, or deadlines
  • Describe how companies respond to mistakes or complaints
  • Use clear and professional verbs to suggest solutions

Let’s dive into two key areas: Workplace Problems and Causes and Solutions and Actions.

1. Workplace Problems and Causes

How do you describe a situation where something goes wrong? These nouns are essential when you want to explain a problem or identify its origin. You’ll use them in reports, emails, or when talking to your manager or colleagues.

Noun Meaning Example
malfunction A machine, system, or device that stops working correctly There was a malfunction in the scanner during the audit.
delay When something happens later than expected The shipment arrived with a 3-day delay.
poor communication When people don’t share or understand information properly The conflict started because of poor communication.
miscommunication Incorrect or unclear information between people A miscommunication caused the wrong file to be sent.
overload Too much work or pressure Many workers are experiencing overload this week.
stress Mental or emotional pressure caused by work Constant stress affects productivity and morale.

Tip: Use “due to” or “because of” to explain causes. Example: The meeting was delayed due to a technical malfunction.

2. Solutions and Actions

Now that you can describe the problems, how do you talk about solving them? These verbs are common in professional communication — from formal reports to team conversations.

Verb Meaning Example
investigate To examine the cause of a problem The manager will investigate the complaint before responding.
resolve To find a solution to a problem We resolved the issue by replacing the broken component.
prevent To stop something from happening Proper training can prevent accidents in the workplace.
report To officially inform someone about a problem Please report any incidents to your supervisor immediately.
replace To change something that is broken or not working The IT team replaced the server after the failure.
compensate To offer something (money, time, service) to fix a problem The company compensated the client for the delivery error.

Tip: Use verbs like investigate, resolve, and compensate in formal writing. Report and replace are common in emails or when giving instructions.

How to Use This Vocabulary

This vocabulary will help you participate in conversations about real work challenges. Try using these words and expressions in:

  • Emails: We’re working to resolve the issue as soon as possible.
  • Meetings: Let’s investigate the cause of the delay.
  • Reports: The malfunction was reported and the part was replaced.
  • Instructions: Report the problem immediately and stop the machine.

You’ll also use this vocabulary in the unit tasks, especially when describing workplace situations or giving advice in writing.
