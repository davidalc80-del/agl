2.1. Vocabulary Focus

📚 Vocabulary – Describing Ethical Challenges at Work

Introduction

In many workplaces, people face difficult situations where they must decide whether to act or stay silent. Sometimes they see unfair treatment, extra invisible work, or clear discrimination. To describe these challenges — and how to respond — we need precise vocabulary.

This section focuses on two things:

  • Key nouns and concepts from the listening (e.g. glass ceiling, bias, burnout)
  • Useful phrasal verbs and expressions to talk about ethical action

✅ Key Concepts for Ethical Challenges

Word Meaning Example
promotion A move to a higher job position She should get a promotion after her excellent work.
non-promotable task Work that doesn’t help career advancement Employees mustn’t focus only on non-promotable tasks like scheduling.
glass ceiling An invisible barrier to career progress, often for women or minorities Companies must address the glass ceiling to ensure fairness.
burnout Extreme exhaustion from stress or overwork Managers should prevent burnout by balancing workloads.
bias Unfair judgment or preference Hiring mustn’t show bias toward specific groups.
fairness Treating everyone equally and justly Leaders ought to prioritize fairness in task assignments.
discrimination Unfair treatment based on traits like gender or race Workplaces mustn’t tolerate discrimination of any kind.
accountability Taking responsibility for your actions Staff should show accountability for ethical errors.
inclusion Making everyone feel valued and involved Teams ought to promote inclusion in all projects.

💡 Tip: Use these terms to describe workplace challenges and ethical solutions.

✅ Phrasal Verbs and Expressions for Ethical Action

🔹 Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal Verb Meaning Example
speak up Voice concerns about something wrong You should speak up about unfair treatment.
stand up for Defend someone treated unfairly She stood up for her colleague against bias.
own up to Admit responsibility for a mistake He must own up to his role in the conflict.
turn a blind eye Ignore a problem deliberately Managers mustn’t turn a blind eye to discrimination.
call out Publicly criticize unethical behavior They called out the unfair promotion process.
step in Intervene to address an issue HR should step in to stop workplace gossip.

🔹 Functional Expressions with Modal Verbs

Expression Use Example
You mustn’t ignore this issue. Strong prohibition You mustn’t ignore this issue of workplace bias.
You should talk to HR. Suggestion or advice You should talk to HR about the discrimination.
They could report it anonymously. Possibility or option They could report the unfairness anonymously.
We ought to do something about it. Moral obligation We ought to do something about the glass ceiling.
Everyone has to take responsibility. General obligation Everyone has to take responsibility for ethical conduct.

💡 Tip: Practice these phrases to suggest or discuss ethical actions.
