2.2. Practice

Interactive Activities – Workplace Values and Listening Task

Instructions: Complete the activities below to practice vocabulary, phrasal verbs, modal expressions, and contextual interpretation related to workplace ethics. For the Listening Task, listen to the provided audio and answer the questions. Use the text inputs or dropdowns to answer, then click "Check Answers" for each activity to see feedback.

✅ Activity 1 – Complete the Sentences

Instructions: Type the correct word from the box (bias, fairness, non-promotable tasks, inclusion, discrimination) to complete each sentence. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 1. Companies must ensure _____ in their hiring and promotion processes.
  • 2. Repeating _____ like organising birthdays won’t help your career.
  • 3. HR should monitor for _____ to make sure everyone feels respected.
  • 4. _____ can happen when people are treated differently for unfair reasons.
  • 5. Promoting _____ helps teams value every member equally.

💡 Tip: Choose the term that best fits the ethical context of each sentence.

✅ Activity 2 – Choose the Correct Phrasal Verb

Instructions: Select the correct phrasal verb to complete each sentence. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 1. If you see someone being excluded, you should _____.
  • 2. The supervisor _____ and stopped the argument before it escalated.
  • 3. The company _____ the unfair treatment of part-time staff.
  • 4. He finally _____ his mistake in the client report.

💡 Tip: Consider the action described in each sentence to choose the correct phrasal verb.

✅ Activity 3 – Modal Expressions: Complete with the Correct Phrase

Instructions: Select the correct modal expression to complete each sentence. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 1. A colleague is experiencing bias. _____.
  • 2. The glass ceiling is still a problem in our department. _____.
  • 3. Managers often miss signs of burnout. _____.
  • 4. If someone is afraid to speak, _____.
  • 5. Ethical behaviour is everyone’s duty. _____.

💡 Tip: Choose the modal expression that best conveys the rule or advice for each situation.

✅ Activity 4 – Choose the Best Option 

Instructions: Select the best option to complete each sentence based on the professional and ethical context. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 1. A company that always promotes men but never women is showing:
  • 2. Repeatedly asking someone to organise meetings or take notes is an example of:
  • 3. A leader who listens to team concerns and treats everyone equally promotes:
  • 4. An employee who admits a mistake and apologises is showing:

💡 Tip: Use the workplace context to select the term that best describes the situation.
