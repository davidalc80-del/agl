1. Listening

🎧 Listening Practice – Promoting Ethical Workplace Values

📝 Introduction

In this activity, you’ll listen to a conversation about fostering an ethical workplace culture. The speakers discuss values like respect, accountability, and fairness, and behaviors to avoid, such as gossip, bias, or discrimination. These are real challenges in many workplaces, and knowing how to talk about them is a key skill for any job.

This listening will help you understand:

  • How teams promote values like integrity and inclusion.
  • Which behaviors can harm workplace trust.
  • How to use precise vocabulary to describe ethical practices.
  • How to spot rules and advice through modal verbs.

🧠 Listening Strategies – What Can Help You?

Before you listen:

  • Read the questions first to focus on key information.
  • Review key vocabulary: respect, trust, gossip, fairness, bias, multitasking, burnout.

While you listen:

  • Listen for examples: Speakers often use real situations to explain ideas.
  • Notice modal verbs like must, should, mustn’t, have to — they signal rules or advice.
  • Don’t stress about every word — focus on main ideas and the speakers’ tone.

After you listen:

  • Check your answers by listening again if needed.
  • Reflect: Which values matter most in your future workplace? Do any examples feel familiar?

💡 Tip: Focus on the speakers’ tone to catch their opinions or feelings.

🎯 Learning Goals

  • Understand a professional dialogue about workplace ethics.
  • Identify key vocabulary in context (e.g., respect, bias, promotion, glass ceiling).
  • Recognize ethical issues and possible solutions in the workplace.

💡 Tip: Listen for examples — they show how speakers explain their points.
