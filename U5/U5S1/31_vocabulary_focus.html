3.1. Vocabulary focus

Vocabulary Focus – Workplace Values and Behaviours

Building an Ethical Workplace

Every workplace strives for fairness and respect, but challenges like unfair treatment or harmful behaviors can undermine these goals. Knowing how to describe positive values and identify problematic behaviors helps you communicate effectively, address issues professionally, and contribute to a better work environment.

In this session, you’ll learn vocabulary to:

  • Express core workplace values that promote fairness and trust.
  • Identify behaviors that harm teams or ethical standards.
  • Discuss ways to foster respect and accountability in professional settings.
  • Use precise terms to describe ethical expectations and challenges.

Let’s dive into two key areas: Core Workplace Values and Problematic Workplace Behaviours.

💡 Tip: Consider how these terms can help you communicate in your vocational field.

✅ Core Workplace Values

Word Meaning Example
respect Treating others with politeness and fairness Team members must show respect in all interactions.
integrity Acting honestly and ethically, even without supervision Leaders should demonstrate integrity in decision-making.
fairness Ensuring equal and unbiased treatment Performance reviews ought to be based on fairness.
equality Providing the same opportunities to everyone Companies should promote equality in hiring practices.
accountability Taking responsibility for one’s actions Employees have to show accountability for their mistakes.
trust Confidence in others’ reliability and honesty Building trust is essential for effective teamwork.
transparency Being open and honest in communication Managers can foster transparency by sharing clear goals.

💡 Tip: Use these words to describe positive actions in your workplace.

⚠️ Problematic Workplace Behaviours

Word Meaning Example
gossip Spreading rumors or talking about others behind their backs Gossip mustn’t be allowed to disrupt team morale.
harassment Unwanted, offensive behaviour toward someone Any form of harassment should be reported immediately.
favouritism Giving unfair advantages to certain individuals Managers mustn’t show favouritism in promotions.
bias Unfair prejudice or preference that distorts judgment Hiring decisions should be free from bias.
discrimination Treating people unfairly based on specific characteristics Discrimination can harm a company’s ethical culture.
negligence Failing to take proper care or responsibility Negligence in following ethical guidelines might lead to conflicts.

💡 Tip: These terms help you identify and discuss behaviours to avoid.

🚀 Apply Your Vocabulary

Reflect on these questions to connect the vocabulary to your professional context:

  • Which value (e.g., respect, integrity) is most important in your vocational field, and why?
  • Have you observed a problematic behaviour like gossip or bias in a workplace or school setting? How could it be addressed?
  • How can you use a term like transparency or accountability to describe an ethical action in your future role?

These reflections strengthen your Competence to Learn to Learn by connecting new vocabulary to real-world applications. Use these words in discussions, reports, or emails to communicate ethically and professionally.

💡 Tip: Practice using one value and one problematic behaviour in a sentence about your workplace.
