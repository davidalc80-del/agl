2.1. Diagnostic Test

Introduction to Diagnostic Test

🎯 Get Ready to Check Your Grammar!

Before we dive into grammar explanations, let’s take a moment to see what you already know. This diagnostic test will check your understanding of three key grammar points used in professional communication about workplace ethics:

  • It is + adjective + to + verb – to give advice and recommendations, like suggesting ethical practices.
  • There is / There are – to present facts or possibilities, such as describing workplace policies.
  • One / We + verb – to speak generally or inclusively, often in formal or ethical discussions.

Imagine you’re advising a team on ethical behavior or describing your company’s values. These grammar structures help you communicate clearly and professionally. This test isn’t for marks—it’s a tool to boost your Competence to Learn to Learn and Competence for Lifelong Learning by identifying your strengths and areas to grow.

💡 Tip: Think of each question as a chance to practice communication in your vocational field.

🚀 What’s the Goal?

The test helps you decide if you need to review these grammar points or if you’re ready to move on to practice. Complete the activities, check your answers with the Answer Key, and calculate your score:

  • Score 80% or higher (16/20): You’re confident with these structures! Skip the grammar explanation and jump to the practice activities to apply your skills.
  • Score below 80%: Take a few minutes to review the grammar explanations—it’ll strengthen your professional communication skills.

This activity builds your Communicative Competence by practicing precise, ethical language and your Professional Competence by preparing you to express values in the workplace. Get started and take control of your learning!

💡 Tip: Read each item carefully, considering how the grammar reflects advice, facts, or general statements in an ethical context.

Diagnostic Test – Modal Verbs in Workplace Ethics

Instructions: Complete the test by choosing the correct modal verb, filling in the blanks, or correcting errors. There are 20 items. Click "Check Answers" for each part to see feedback. At the end, check your score. If you score 80% or higher (16/20), you can skip this grammar point.

Part A – Multiple Choice: Choose the Correct Modal

Instructions: Select the modal verb that best fits the sentence based on workplace ethics. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 1. Employees _____ report unethical behavior to maintain integrity.
  • 2. You _____ gossip about colleagues, as it harms team trust.
  • 3. New hires _____ complete mandatory ethics training.
  • 4. We _____ hold workshops to discuss workplace fairness.
  • 5. Ignoring minor issues _____ lead to a damaged company reputation.
  • 6. Employees _____ suggest improvements to the ethical guidelines.
  • 7. Mentors _____ guide younger workers to make fair decisions.

💡 Tip: Choose the modal that reflects the speaker’s stance—e.g., necessity (must, have to), prohibition (mustn’t), or possibility (could, might).

Part B – Sentence Completion

Instructions: Type the correct modal verb to complete each sentence about workplace ethics. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 8. Managers _____ create a safe space for ethical discussions.
  • 9. You _____ think ethics only matters in formal policies.
  • 10. Employees _____ attend optional diversity seminars, but they’re encouraged to.
  • 11. Companies _____ promote daily conversations about values.
  • 12. A small oversight _____ cause significant ethical issues later.
  • 13. Staff _____ report harassment anonymously if they feel uncomfortable.
  • 14. We _____ organize a meeting to address workplace conflicts.

💡 Tip: Use modals that match the ethical context—e.g., strong duty (must), recommendation (should), or possibility (might).

Part C – Find and Correct the Mistake

Instructions: Each sentence has one modal verb error. Type the corrected sentence in the text box. Click "Check Answers" for feedback.

  • 15. ❌ You can ignore complaints about favoritism in the workplace.
  • 16. ❌ Managers might create a safe space for ethical discussions.
  • 17. ❌ Employees don’t have to report unethical behavior to HR.
  • 18. ❌ We must discuss values only during annual training.
  • 19. ❌ Mentors can guide younger workers to make fair decisions.
  • 20. ❌ A small ethical lapse have to damage the company’s culture.

💡 Tip: Look for modal verbs that don’t match the ethical urgency or context, such as incorrect necessity or possibility.
