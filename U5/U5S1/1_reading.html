📖 Unit 5 – Session 1: Introduction to Reading Activity

🎯 What Does Ethics Mean to You?

Imagine you’re starting your dream job. You’re skilled, motivated, and ready to make a difference. But what happens when you face a tough choice—like whether to speak up about a mistake or treat everyone fairly under pressure? Ethics isn’t just about rules; it’s about building a workplace where values like integrity and respect shine through every day.

In this session, you’ll read “Building an Ethical Company,” a text that reveals how workplaces can nurture ethical professionals. Before you dive in, let’s spark your thinking with a few questions to connect the topic to your own experiences and future career.

  • What does “being ethical” mean to you in a workplace? Think of one value (e.g., honesty, fairness) that matters most in your vocational field.
  • Have you ever seen or heard about an unethical situation at work or school, like gossip or favoritism? How did it affect the environment?
  • Why might young professionals need support to make ethical decisions? Consider a time you felt unsure about the “right” thing to do.

💡 Tip: Jot down your thoughts—these questions will help you connect with the reading’s ideas.

🚀 Ready to Explore?

Get ready to explore how companies can create ethical cultures through daily practices, mentoring, and open discussions. The reading will show you practical ways to make ethics a core part of your professional life, no matter your role. As you read, think about how these strategies could apply to your own career path.

💡 Tip: Skim the text first to capture the main message, then read closely for details that inspire you.

Reading Text – Building an Ethical Company

Adapted from Harvard Business Review



What does it mean to have an ethical company? Some people think ethics is just about following the rules, but it’s more than that. A company should not only avoid bad behaviour — it should also help people do the right thing. According to researchers Isaac H. Smith and Maryam Kouchaki, a workplace should be a place where employees can learn and grow as ethical professionals.

People are not born with fixed values. We learn values like respect, fairness or integrity by working with others and facing real situations. That’s why companies should talk about values every day, not only during training. Managers and leaders must help their teams understand what is right and what is wrong. For example, they ought to create a safe space where people feel comfortable to speak and reflect.

Work is a big part of life. You have to make good decisions not just when someone is watching, but all the time. You mustn’t think that ethics only matters in official documents. It matters in meetings, in emails, and in daily conversations.

Young workers or people with little experience may not know how to react in difficult situations. That’s normal. Companies should support them by giving examples, asking questions, and organising discussions about real problems. For example, they can talk about harassment, favouritism, or how to stop gossip at work. These are all behaviours that can damage a company’s culture.

Also, mentoring is a good way to share experience. A mentor ought to help younger workers think about their decisions and how to treat others with equality and accountability. If mentors don’t talk about values, the company loses an important opportunity to teach and support.

In short, if a company wants to build a positive and professional environment, it must include ethics in everyday life — not just in rules, but in actions.

💡 Tip: Read the text carefully to understand the key ideas about workplace ethics.
