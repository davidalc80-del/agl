2. Use of English

Use of English – Modal Verbs in Workplace Ethics

Welcome to Use of English!

In any workplace, communicating expectations, responsibilities, and ethical advice clearly and respectfully is essential. Modal verbs help you express what’s required, what’s forbidden, or what’s recommended when discussing professional values. In this section, you’ll explore how to use modal verbs to express:

  • Obligation – e.g., “You must report unethical behavior.”
  • Prohibition – e.g., “You mustn’t share confidential information.”
  • Advice and Recommendation – e.g., “We should promote fairness daily.”
  • Possibility and Permission – e.g., “Staff can suggest ethical improvements.”

We’ll draw examples from the reading text “Building an Ethical Company” and apply these verbs in professional scenarios, focusing on values like respect, equality, and accountability. This section is divided into three steps:

  • Diagnostic Test – Check your current understanding of modal verbs.
  • Grammar Lesson – Learn and reflect with real-world examples.
  • Grammar Practice – Consolidate your skills through guided activities.

💡 Tip: Think about how modal verbs shape ethical communication in your vocational field.

🎯 Why This Matters

Using modal verbs effectively will help you:

  • Communicate workplace rules and ethical advice with clarity and confidence.
  • Adapt to professional contexts, such as meetings or reports.
  • Express values like integrity and fairness in a precise, respectful way.

This section builds your Communicative Competence by enhancing your ability to express obligations and recommendations, Professional Competence by preparing you for ethical workplace communication, and Competence to Learn to Learn through self-assessment and targeted practice. Ready? Begin with the diagnostic test!

💡 Tip: Use the diagnostic test to identify areas for improvement in your grammar skills.
