The Future of Work: Skills, Change and Adaptation

The world of work is changing fast. New technologies, new ways of working, and new employee expectations are transforming companies everywhere. Every day, people learn new skills to stay up to date, and companies look for new solutions to prepare their workers.

Experts say the most important thing for the future is skills. In the past, many companies looked only at degrees. But now, they want people who can do specific things. In the next five years, many jobs will change, and 44% of worker skills will be disrupted, according to a report by the World Economic Forum.

So what are companies doing about it? Many companies have a plan: they are going to offer training to help workers improve. Some companies are investing in new systems to evaluate what each employee can do. They are creating learning programs that connect directly to real jobs.

At the same time, some things are already changing. For example, some businesses are hiring new specialists who focus only on skills. Other companies are using AI (artificial intelligence) as part of the team. By 2027, AI will do around 40% of the tasks in many companies. But AI cannot do everything. Humans and machines will need to work together.

For workers, training is also changing. Now, many people don‚Äôt go to long classes. Instead, they learn in small steps during the working day. Imagine this: a worker is starting a new task. A video appears on their tablet, showing them exactly what to do. This is called micro-training.

In the future, companies will continue to change. Traditional jobs might disappear, and new jobs will appear. Employees are learning to adapt, and employers are creating systems to help them. The companies that move fast and support their people will succeed.

Change is coming ‚Äî but with the right skills and support, it can be a good thing.

References: Adapted from Forbes, June 28, 2023; TieTalent, undated.
